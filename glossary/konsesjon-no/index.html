Konsesjon (Purchase Licence / Concession)

Konsesjon is a government licence required to purchase certain Norwegian properties — particularly agricultural land, forests, and rural properties above certain size or value thresholds. Foreign buyers face additional requirements.

Norway's Concession Act (konsesjonsloven) requires buyers of certain property types to obtain prior approval from the local municipality before a purchase can be completed. The purpose is to control land use, protect agricultural land, and prevent speculative purchasing that would price out local residents in rural areas.

Konsesjon is required for agricultural and forestry properties regardless of buyer nationality. For residential rural properties, the requirement is triggered when a property exceeds 100 decares (10 hectares) in total area, or when the residential plot exceeds 25 decares and the price is above a statutory threshold. In practice, most standard urban and suburban homes do not require konsesjon.

Foreign nationals from outside the EEA do not enjoy freedom to purchase Norwegian property without restriction. EEA citizens generally have the same rights as Norwegian citizens for residential property, but non-EEA buyers may face additional scrutiny and conditions.

The konsesjon application is assessed on factors including the buyer's intention to live on the property, the price paid (assessed against the agricultural value), and the buyer's willingness to maintain the property's primary use. Applications can be refused if the price is deemed too high relative to agricultural utility.

Violation of the konsesjon requirement — completing a purchase without required approval — can result in the municipality demanding the property be sold within a set period, typically at a price determined by the authority.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Never assume an agricultural property does not need konsesjon because it has a residential building on it. The presence of farmland or forest attached to the property almost always triggers the requirement.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: If you buy a farm property and self-declare exemption (egenerklaring) by committing to live there for at least five years, ensure you understand the consequences of moving away before the five-year period ends — it triggers full konsesjon review.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do city apartments require konsesjon?

No. Standard urban and suburban residential properties do not require konsesjon. The requirement is almost exclusively relevant for agricultural land and large rural plots.

How long does the konsesjon process take?

Typically 4–12 weeks depending on the municipality's workload. The purchase contract is usually made conditional on receiving konsesjon within an agreed timeframe.

Can konsesjon be refused?

Yes. The most common grounds for refusal are a price deemed too high above agricultural value, or inadequate plans to maintain the land's agricultural function.
