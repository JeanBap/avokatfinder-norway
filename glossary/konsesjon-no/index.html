Konsesjon

Konsesjon Norway – Property Acquisition Licence for Foreigners and Rural Land

Konsesjon (acquisition licence) is an administrative permit required under Norwegian law (Konsesjonsloven — the Concession Act) for certain property purchases. The most common situations requiring konsesjon are: (1) acquisition of agricultural or forestry land; (2) acquisition of certain rural residential properties; and (3) some acquisitions by foreigners, depending on the property type and location.

For most urban property purchases (apartments, houses in built-up areas), konsesjon is not required by foreign buyers. However, foreigners purchasing holiday homes (fritidseiendommer) in rural areas, agricultural land, or properties above a certain size threshold must apply for konsesjon from the local municipality (kommune). Failure to obtain required konsesjon makes the purchase voidable.

For agricultural land, the konsesjon application is assessed against multiple factors: whether the applicant will farm the land themselves (boplikt — residence obligation), the price (compared to the municipality's assessed agricultural value), and the use of the land. Political decisions at the municipal level significantly influence konsesjon outcomes.

The konsesjon application is submitted to the local municipality and processing takes several months. Buyers typically make the purchase contract conditional on konsesjon being granted within a specified period. If konsesjon is refused, the purchase contract lapses and the deposit must be returned.

In addition to konsesjon, some rural properties carry boplikt (mandatory residence obligation) — a requirement that the owner actually lives on the property as their primary residence. This applies to certain agricultural properties and limits the ability to use them as investment or holiday properties.

⚠ Common MistakeAssuming urban property purchases are always konsesjon-free as a foreigner. While typically true for EEA nationals, the rules vary by property type — always verify before committing.
💡 Expert TipInclude a konsesjonsbetingelse (consesjon condition) in any purchase contract for property where konsesjon may be required. This protects your deposit if the application is refused.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do EEA citizens need konsesjon in Norway?

EEA nationals have equivalent rights to Norwegian citizens for most property purchases. However, for agricultural land and certain rural properties, the konsesjon requirement applies regardless of nationality.

How long does a konsesjon remain valid?

Konsesjon is granted for the specific purchase and purchaser. It is not transferable to a subsequent buyer, who would need to apply independently.

Is there a right of appeal if konsesjon is refused?

Yes. Refusals can be appealed to the Statsforvalteren (County Governor). Further appeal to the Ministry of Agriculture and Food is possible in some cases.
