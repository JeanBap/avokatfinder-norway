Eiendomsskatt

Eiendomsskatt Norway – Municipal Property Tax Explained

Eiendomsskatt (property tax) in Norway is a municipal tax that can optionally be levied by communes on real property within their boundaries. Not all Norwegian municipalities charge it — each municipality decides independently whether to impose it and at what rate, within national guidelines. This creates significant variation in property holding costs between locations.

Where charged, eiendomsskatt is calculated as a percentage of the property's assessed tax value (beregningsgrunnlag), which is set by the municipality (often based on national Skatteetaten property valuations). The rate must be between 1‰ and 7‰ (per mille) of the assessed value. Some municipalities apply a basic deduction (bunnfradrag) reducing the tax base for primary residences.

Major Norwegian cities including Oslo, Bergen, and Trondheim have historically charged eiendomsskatt, though Oslo exempted primary residences for a period before reintroducing limited rates. The political dynamics around eiendomsskatt are active — buyers should verify the current situation in the relevant municipality at the time of purchase.

For buyers, eiendomsskatt is an ongoing holding cost that reduces investment yield. Its impact is greatest for properties with high assessed values in high-rate municipalities. A NOK 5 million property in a 7‰ municipality without a bunnfradrag would face an annual eiendomsskatt of NOK 35,000.

Eiendomsskatt is the responsibility of the property owner as of 1 January each year. Buyers purchasing during the year should clarify in the purchase contract how any prepaid or underpaid eiendomsskatt is allocated. The seller's obligation to the municipality runs to year-end but the apportionment between buyer and seller is typically handled by agreement.

⚠ Common MistakeAssuming eiendomsskatt applies uniformly across Norway. Municipalities vary significantly — some charge 7‰ without any deduction, others charge nothing. Always verify the current rate for the specific municipality.
💡 Expert TipCheck the municipality's eiendomsskatt page before making an offer on a property. Many municipalities publish their rates and assessed values online. For investment properties, factor the ongoing tax into your yield model.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I appeal my eiendomsskatt assessment?

Yes. Norwegian property owners have the right to appeal the assessed tax value (beregningsgrunnlag) to the municipality within a set period after the assessment notice is issued.

Does eiendomsskatt apply to borettslag apartments?

It applies to the borettslag (as the property owner), not directly to individual members. The cost is typically passed through the fellesutgifter.

Is eiendomsskatt deductible against rental income?

Yes, for rental properties, eiendomsskatt is a deductible operating expense against Norwegian rental income.
