Meglerprovisjon Norway – Estate Agent Commission Structure

Meglerprovisjon (estate agent commission) is the fee paid to a licensed Norwegian estate agent (eiendomsmegler) for conducting a property sale. It is paid by the seller and typically ranges from 1% to 3% of the sale price, though flat-fee alternatives exist for simpler transactions. In major cities, total costs including commission, marketing, and administration can reach NOK 80,000–200,000 for a typical apartment sale.

Norwegian estate agents are licensed by the Financial Supervisory Authority of Norway (Finanstilsynet) under the Eiendomsmeglingsloven (Estate Agency Act). Like Swedish agents, Norwegian agents have a duty to act in the interests of both parties (nøytralitetsplikt) rather than exclusively representing the seller. They must disclose all known material facts about the property to buyers.

In addition to the base meglerprovisjon, sellers typically pay for: photography and styling (markedsføring), boligsalgsrapport (property condition report prepared by an independent surveyor), and any advertising on Finn.no. These additional costs can add NOK 30,000–80,000 to the total agency cost. Buyers should always request an itemised total cost estimate from agents.

The boligsalgsrapport — a condition survey prepared by an independent takstmann (building inspector) — is standard in Norwegian property sales and is provided to buyers as part of the sales documentation. Since 2022, the legal standard for sellers' disclosure obligations has tightened, and buyers are expected to read the boligsalgsrapport carefully before bidding.

Since the 2022 legal changes, sellers bear responsibility for undisclosed defects more clearly than before, and the boligsalgsrapport must be prepared by a qualified inspector following specific standards. This has reduced post-sale disputes but increased pre-sale costs for sellers.

⚠ Common MistakeNot requesting an itemised total cost quote from the agent. The meglerprovisjon percentage understates total agency costs; a 1.5% meglerprovisjon can become 2.5% effective rate once photography, marketing, and boligsalgsrapport costs are included.
💡 Expert TipNegotiate the meglerprovisjon before signing the oppdragsavtale (agent mandate). Sellers in competitive markets have more leverage than they often realise. Request quotes from at least three agents and compare total package costs, not just the commission percentage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I sell a Norwegian property without an agent?

Yes, though it is uncommon for private sales (selge selv). Buyers tend to trust agent-listed properties more, and the agent provides the legal documentation (kontrakt) and the boligsalgsrapport framework.

Who pays the agent if the sale falls through?

The seller typically owes the agent's expenses (utlegg) even if the sale does not complete, but the commission component is usually only due on a successful sale.

How is the meglerprovisjon VAT treated?

VAT at 25% applies to the meglerprovisjon. This is a significant additional cost: a 2% commission on NOK 4 million = NOK 80,000 + NOK 20,000 VAT = NOK 100,000 total.
