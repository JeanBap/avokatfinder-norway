Oppdragsavtale

Oppdragsavtale Norway – Estate Agent Mandate Agreement

An oppdragsavtale (agency mandate agreement) is the written contract between a Norwegian property seller and their chosen eiendomsmegler (estate agent), specifying the terms of the agency relationship. Under the Eiendomsmeglingsloven, an oppdragsavtale must be in writing and contain specific mandatory information — verbal agency agreements are not permitted.

Mandatory contents include: the agent's name and licence number, the property description, the agreed meglerprovisjon and all other costs, the scope of services, the agreed marketing strategy, the exclusive period (oppdragstid) of maximum six months, and any agreed minimum price (prisgrense). Both parties sign before the property is marketed.

Norwegian law gives sellers a cooling-off right: the oppdragsavtale can be cancelled without penalty within 14 days of signing (the angrefrist under the Angrerettloven). After this period, cancelling the agreement may entitle the agent to recover documented expenses (utlegg), and in some cases a portion of the commission if marketing has begun.

Key Facts

FormMust be written — oral agreements invalid
Mandatory contentsCommission, services, costs, period, property details
Cancellation right14 days angrefrist (cooling-off)
Maximum period6 months (oppdragstid)
After periodSeller free to re-instruct different agent

The oppdragstid (mandate period) cannot exceed six months. If the property has not sold within this period, the seller can choose to renew with the same agent or switch agents without paying the original agent's commission on a subsequent sale — unless the buyer was introduced by the original agent during their mandate period.

The oppdragsavtale sets the terms for how the budrunde will be conducted and what the agent's obligations are to both buyer and seller. Sellers should negotiate marketing budget, minimum price, and the commission structure (percentage vs flat fee) carefully before signing.

⚠ Common MistakeNot reviewing all costs listed in the oppdragsavtale beyond the commission percentage. Additional photography, styling, advertising, and administration fees can significantly increase the total agency cost.
💡 Expert TipDuring the 14-day angrefrist, review the oppdragsavtale carefully for any clauses that restrict your ability to switch agents or set a minimum price. The mandate period and the scope of services are the most important negotiating points.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I instruct multiple agents simultaneously?

Norwegian oppdragsavtaler are typically exclusive — one agent at a time. Dual mandates (parallell oppdrag) are permitted but create complications in the budrunde and for commission calculations.

What happens if the agent finds a buyer after the mandate expires?

If the buyer was first introduced during the mandate period, the original agent may still be entitled to commission on a subsequent sale to that buyer.

Is the oppdragsavtale a fixed-price commitment?

No. The costs in the oppdragsavtale are estimates and caps — actual marketing costs may be lower if fewer advertising runs are needed.
