Fellesgjeld

Fellesgjeld Norway – Shared Cooperative Debt in Borettslag

Fellesgjeld (shared debt) is the aggregate debt of a borettslag (Norwegian housing cooperative), allocated across all member units in proportion to their size or share value. It is the single most important financial metric to understand when buying a borettslag apartment in Norway, as it directly determines the true total purchase cost.

The fellesgjeld appears separately from the advertised sale price (prisantydning) on all Norwegian property listings on Finn.no. The totalpris (total price) — sale price plus buyer's share of fellesgjeld — represents the true economic cost of the purchase. Norwegian mortgage lenders assess affordability based on totalpris, not just the andel price.

Many borettslag have taken advantage of the IS-lån (Interest Only Loan) structure, where the cooperative services only interest on the fellesgjeld during the IS-period. When the IS-period ends, the loan converts to a repayment mortgage, increasing monthly fees substantially for all members. Buyers of borettslag with large IS-lån must understand the timeline for conversion.

Key Facts

DefinitionCollective borettslag debt, buyer's share allocated per unit
DisclosureListed separately on Finn.no (totalpris = price + fellesgjeld)
IS-lån riskFee increase when interest-only period ends
LenderBank or Husbanken holds as collective obligation
Rate sensitivityHigh fellesgjeld = high interest rate exposure

The borettslag's lender (typically a major Norwegian bank or Husbanken) holds the fellesgjeld as a collective obligation. If the cooperative defaults, the lender can ultimately seek possession of the building — a risk for individual members that argues strongly for checking the borettslag's financial health before purchase.

Recent interest rate rises in Norway significantly increased fellesutgifter for high-fellesgjeld cooperatives. Borettslag with fellesgjeld per unit exceeding NOK 1 million have seen monthly fee increases of 30–50% in some cases. Buyers should stress-test their budget against continued rate increases.

⚠ Common MistakeBudgeting only for the andel purchase price without including the fellesgjeld share. A buyer with a NOK 3m andel budget and NOK 1.5m fellesgjeld actually has only NOK 1.5m for the purchase price portion.
💡 Expert TipAsk the borettslag's forretningsfører (manager) for the complete loan schedule, including IS-periods and conversion dates. This is not always obvious from the sales prospectus but is critical for long-term cost planning.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is fellesgjeld fixed for the life of my ownership?

No. The borettslag can take on additional loans for major works, increasing the fellesgjeld. Board decisions and generalforsamling votes on new borrowing should be monitored.

Does fellesgjeld count against my mortgage borrowing limit?

Yes. Norwegian banks include your allocated share of fellesgjeld when calculating total debt against the 5× income mortgage limit. High fellesgjeld reduces the amount you can borrow for the andel purchase.

Can a borettslag eliminate its fellesgjeld?

Yes. Members can vote to pay down the fellesgjeld collectively or individually. Some cooperatives have organised group refinancing through Husbanken to reduce rates.
