Eierseksjon

Eierseksjon Norway – Condominium Ownership (Sameie)

An eierseksjon is an independently owned residential or commercial unit within a building where each section owner holds full real property title to their unit and a proportional share of the common areas (sameie). Unlike a borettslag (cooperative), there is no collective debt by default — each owner finances their unit independently and the sameie typically carries no collective borrowing.

Eierseksjoner are governed by the Eierseksjonsloven (Condominium Act) and registered in the national property register (Grunnboken) at Kartverket. Each eierseksjon has its own title entry (grunnboksblad), making it full freehold property in the legal sense. Title transfer is completed by deed registration (tinglysing) at Kartverket.

Owners of eierseksjoner form a sameie (owners' association). The sameie manages common areas, arranges building insurance, and maintains a renovation fund. Decisions are made by vote at the annual general meeting; major decisions require qualified majority. Monthly fees (felleskostnader) cover common maintenance and insurance but are typically lower than borettslag fees since there is no collective debt.

Key Facts

OwnershipFull real property title per unit
Collective debtTypically none (unlike borettslag)
RegistrationGrunnboken at Kartverket (tinglysing)
ManagementSeksjonssameiet — felleskostnader
Key documentSeksjonserklæring (division declaration)

When purchasing an eierseksjon, buyers review the seksjonssameiet's accounts and renovation plan (vedlikeholdsplan). A poorly maintained building with an underfunded renovation reserve will require special levies (ekstraordinære innbetalinger). These are a risk comparable to fellesgjeld in borettslag.

The key due diligence check is the seksjoneringsbegjæring (the original application to divide the building into sections) and the current seksjonserklæring (declaration of division), which defines each unit's boundaries and the allocation of common areas. Any disputes about unit boundaries or exclusive use areas are resolved by reference to these documents.

⚠ Common MistakeAssuming no shared costs exist because there is no fellesgjeld. Eierseksjoner have felleskostnader for maintenance and insurance, and the sameie can levy special contributions for major works.
💡 Expert TipRequest the sameiet's full vedlikeholdsplan and financial accounts before committing to purchase. Ask specifically whether any major works are planned in the next five years — this is often disclosed only in meeting minutes, not in the sales prospectus.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between eierseksjon and borettslag?

An eierseksjon gives full real property ownership with individual financing. A borettslag gives a cooperative share with collective debt. The main practical difference for buyers is the fellesgjeld in borettslag.

Can an eierseksjon be mortgaged?

Yes. Banks lend against eierseksjoner as full real property. The mortgage is registered as a pant (lien) in Grunnboken.

How long does tinglysing (registration) take for an eierseksjon sale?

Kartverket processes tinglysing within a few days for electronic submissions (most are now electronic). Paper submissions take longer.
