Prisantydning (Asking Price / Price Indication)

Prisantydning is the non-binding asking price set by the seller and their estate agent for a Norwegian property. It is a marketing figure, not a binding offer, and sales almost always conclude above it in competitive markets.

In Norwegian property listings, the prisantydning is simply the price the seller hopes to achieve — a starting point for the budrunde (bidding round), not a fixed price. Unlike markets where listing prices are negotiated down, the Norwegian auction system typically pushes final prices above the prisantydning.

Estate agents are professionally obligated to advise sellers on a realistic prisantydning based on comparable sales (sammenlignbare salg). Setting it too high discourages bidders; setting it deliberately low to attract a bidding war (a practice sometimes called underprising) can be controversial but is not illegal.

Published statistics from Eiendom Norge track the average ratio of final sale price to prisantydning. In Oslo, this ratio has historically ranged from 100–110%, meaning buyers typically pay 0–10% above the asking price. In slower regional markets the ratio may be at or below 100%.

The finn.no portal — Norway's dominant property listing site — shows both the prisantydning and the final sold price (salgspris) for completed sales. Studying this data for comparable properties in the same neighbourhood gives buyers a realistic bidding budget.

Costs above the prisantydning that buyers must budget for include: the buyer's proportional share of fellesgjeld (in a borettslag), the omkoestninger (transaction costs including agent fees, document fees, and any stamp duty analogue), and the cost of financing pre-approval. The totalpris concept — unit price plus all associated debt — is the number that truly matters.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Budgeting only to the prisantydning in a competitive Oslo neighbourhood will leave you repeatedly outbid. Research recent salgspris data and budget 5–10% above asking in hot areas.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Use finn.no's historical sold-price data (select 'solgt' in the search filters) to build a realistic view of what properties actually trade at in your target streets — this is free and publicly accessible.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the prisantydning negotiable before the budrunde starts?

Yes, but rare. If a property has been sitting unsold for weeks, you may approach the seller privately. In a normal market, the budrunde is the standard mechanism.

What are omkoestninger?

Transaction costs the buyer pays on top of the purchase price, including the estate agent's buyer's fee (if any), land registry fees, and document stamp duty (dokumentavgift) on freehold properties.

Does dokumentavgift apply to borettslag purchases?

No. Dokumentavgift (2.5% of property value) applies only to freehold properties (selveier). Borettslag andel transfers are not subject to dokumentavgift.
