Eierskifteforsikring (Seller's Title Insurance)

Eierskifteforsikring is an insurance policy taken out by the property seller in Norway that covers the buyer's claims for hidden defects discovered after purchase — it does not protect the seller, despite its name.

Despite being called 'seller's insurance', eierskifteforsikring primarily benefits the buyer. When a seller takes out this policy before selling, they transfer the financial risk of post-sale hidden-defect claims to the insurer. The buyer can then pursue the insurer directly for compensation rather than having to chase the seller personally.

Norwegian property is sold 'as is' (som den er), meaning the seller can disclaim liability for defects — but this disclaimer does not apply to defects the seller knew about and concealed, or to defects that the seller ought to have known about given their period of ownership. Eierskifteforsikring covers these latent liability situations.

The insurance is closely linked to the condition survey (tilstandsrapport / boligsalgsrapport). The surveyor grades elements of the property using a traffic-light system (TG0–TG3). The insurer uses this report to assess the risk they are underwriting. A property with multiple TG3 ratings (serious defects) will result in a higher premium or exclusions.

From 2022, regulation changed to make the tilstandsrapport mandatory for residential sales and tightened the surveyor's liability. This reform was intended to reduce disputes by creating a clear, standardised baseline at the point of sale.

Buyers should still commission their own independent survey — the tilstandsrapport is not a guarantee of quality, only a snapshot of visible conditions at a point in time. Hidden defects by definition are not visible at inspection.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Eierskifteforsikring does not cover defects that were clearly visible at the time of sale and noted in the condition report. Read the boligsalgsrapport carefully — anything rated TG3 was disclosed and is typically excluded.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: As a buyer, still commission your own independent survey from a building engineer separate from the seller's boligsalgsrapport. The seller's surveyor is instructed by the seller, not by you.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I claim directly against the insurance company?

Yes. If the seller has eierskifteforsikring, you submit your claim to their insurer. You do not need to sue the seller personally first.

What is a TG rating?

Tilstandsgrad (TG) is a 0–3 scale used by Norwegian surveyors. TG0 = no defect, TG1 = minor, TG2 = significant, TG3 = serious/immediate action needed.

Is eierskifteforsikring compulsory?

No. Sellers can sell without it, but most estate agents strongly recommend it because uninsured sellers face unlimited personal liability for hidden defects discovered post-sale.
