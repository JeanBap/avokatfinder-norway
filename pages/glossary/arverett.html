Arverett

Definition: Norwegian legal right to inherit property and assets from a deceased person's estate.

Overview

Arverett is the legal right granted to family members and other beneficiaries to inherit from a deceased person's estate under Norwegian law. The right to inherit is established through either a valid will or statutory succession rules.

Norwegian succession law provides a hierarchy of heirs: spouses, children, parents, and more distant relatives inherit in a prescribed order if no valid will exists. The surviving spouse has special protections and often inherits a significant portion.

A person's arverett can be exercised by accepting the inheritance within a specified period, or by renouncing it if the estate has debts or liabilities. This choice must be made deliberately and cannot be undone.

Under Norwegian law, a child born outside marriage has the same arverett as a legitimate child. Additionally, adopted children acquire full arverett from their adoptive parents.

The exercise of arverett may be limited if the deceased left specific instructions in a valid will. In such cases, beneficiaries must follow the testator's wishes regarding asset distribution.

Key Facts

  • Establishes right to inherit under Norwegian law
  • Granted through will or statutory succession rules
  • Spouse, children, parents inherit in statutory order
  • Must be accepted or renounced within set period
  • Can be limited or directed by valid will

Common Mistake

Watch out: Accepting an inheritance without verifying the estate's debts, potentially inheriting liabilities that exceed assets.

Pro Tip

💡 Consult a Norwegian lawyer before accepting a large inheritance to understand tax implications and potential debts.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if someone dies in Norway without a will?

Norwegian law applies statutory succession rules, distributing the estate to surviving spouse, children, and other relatives in a prescribed order.

Can I refuse an inheritance in Norway?

Yes, you can renounce (fraski) your arverett if you wish to decline the inheritance, typically within a 6-month period.

Do adopted children have arverett?

Yes, adopted children have the same inheritance rights as biological children in Norway.
