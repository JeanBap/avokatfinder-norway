Pantoboken

Definition: Norwegian register of mortgages and security interests registered against real property.

Overview

Pantoboken is Norway's centralized register of all mortgages and security interests (panterettigheter) registered against real property. It is maintained by the Land Registry (Statens Kartverk) as part of the overall property registration system.

When a property owner pledges real estate as security for a loan, the mortgage is registered in the pantoboken. This registration secures the lender's claim and gives them priority rights in case of the borrower's default or bankruptcy.

The pantoboken operates on a priority basis: mortgages registered first have priority over later mortgages. This system allows multiple lenders to hold security interests in the same property, with clear rules about which lender gets paid first from sale proceeds.

In case of borrower default, a lender with a registered pantedokument can initiate foreclosure proceedings. The property is sold, and proceeds are distributed according to priority: first mortgage gets paid first, second mortgage second, and so on.

All pantedokuments must comply with Norwegian law requirements and must be properly documented and executed. The Register maintains detailed records to ensure transparency and protect both borrowers and lenders.

Key Facts

  • Register of all mortgages against Norwegian property
  • Maintained by Land Registry (Statens Kartverk)
  • Mortgages rank by registration order (priority)
  • Lenders can foreclose if borrower defaults
  • All mortgages must be properly documented

Common Mistake

Watch out: Taking out multiple mortgages without understanding priority and your remaining equity in the property.

Pro Tip

💡 Review your pantedokument carefully and maintain regular contact with lenders about payment status and terms.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I have multiple mortgages registered in the pantoboken?

Yes. Multiple mortgages can be registered, each with a priority ranking based on registration date.

What happens if my property is foreclosed?

The property is sold, and proceeds go first to the first-priority mortgage, then to later mortgages, with remainder to the owner.

How do I remove a mortgage from the pantoboken?

Once the secured loan is paid in full, the lender must formally discharge (slett) the mortgage from the register.
